  3. Add client details
  4. Add invoice items, quantities, and prices
  5. Set tax rate if applicable
  6. Preview your invoice in real-time

Yes, InvoiceCompass offers several customization options. You can add your company logo, change colors, and select different templates to match your brand identity. All customization options are available in the invoice creator interface.

To add your logo to the invoice, look for the "Add Logo" option in the company information section of the invoice creator. Click on it to upload an image file from your computer. Supported formats include JPG, PNG, and SVG.

Currently, InvoiceCompass doesn't offer cloud storage for invoices. However, you can download your invoices as PDF files and save them on your computer or cloud storage service. This approach ensures you have complete control over your invoice data.

How do I send an invoice to my client?

After generating your invoice PDF, you can send it to your client via email as an attachment. You can also print the PDF for physical delivery or upload it to a client portal if you use one.

Invoice Content Questions

What should I include in my invoice?

A professional invoice should include:

  • Your business name, address, and contact information
  • Your client's name and address
  • A unique invoice number
  • Invoice date and payment due date
  • Detailed description of products or services
  • Quantity, unit price, and total for each item
  • Subtotal, tax (if applicable), and total amount due
  • Payment terms and accepted payment methods
  • Any additional notes or terms and conditions

InvoiceCompass includes fields for all these essential elements.

How should I number my invoices?

Invoice numbers should be unique and follow a consistent format. Common approaches include:

  • Sequential numbers (e.g., 001, 002, 003)
  • Year-based numbers (e.g., 2025-001, 2025-002)
  • Client-based prefixes (e.g., ABC-001 for client ABC)
  • Date-based numbers (e.g., 20250428-001 for April 28, 2025)

Choose a system that works for your business and stick with it for consistency.

Do I need to include tax on my invoice?

Whether you need to include tax depends on your business location, tax registration status, and the nature of your products or services. In many jurisdictions, registered businesses must collect and remit sales tax, VAT, or GST.

InvoiceCompass includes a tax field where you can add the appropriate tax rate. For specific tax advice, consult with a tax professional familiar with your jurisdiction.

What payment terms should I use?

Common payment terms include:

  • Due on receipt: Payment is expected immediately
  • Net 15/30/60: Payment is due within 15, 30, or 60 days
  • 2/10 Net 30: 2% discount if paid within 10 days, otherwise full payment due in 30 days
  • 50% upfront: Partial payment before work begins, remainder upon completion

Choose terms that align with your cash flow needs and industry standards. Be clear about your terms on the invoice to avoid confusion.

What file format are the invoices downloaded in?

Invoices are generated and downloaded as PDF (Portable Document Format) files. PDF is the industry standard for invoices as it preserves formatting across all devices and can be easily shared, printed, or archived.
